Transforming Supply Chain Operations for the New Normal

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

The use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the supply chain is becoming increasingly prevalent as organizations seek to improve speed and efficiency in decision-making, cycle times, operations, and continuous improvement. According to Gartner, the level of machine automation in supply chain processes is expected to double in the next five years, while global spending on IIoT Platforms is predicted to grow to $12.44B in 2024, at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 40% in seven years.

In today's digital world, businesses are striving to maximize productivity by reducing uncertainties, and AI is becoming an essential tool in achieving this goal. Faster operational efficiencies and supersonic speed are also becoming key requirements across industries, further emphasizing the need to leverage AI capabilities in logistics and supply chain management.

Accelerating Supply Chain Success with AI in Supply Chains & Logistics

Utilizing AI technology in supply chains can bring about significant optimization capabilities to accurately plan capacity, enhance demand forecasting, increase productivity, reduce supply chain expenses, and boost output, all while ensuring safer working environments. The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the severe consequences of uncertainties in supply chains, underscoring the necessity for intelligent contingency plans to tackle them effectively. The question remains: is AI the solution to these challenges? What impact can it have on companies striving to stabilize their supply chain and logistics operations? Let's explore further.

1.ACCURATE INVENTORY MANAGEMENT

Efficient inventory management is crucial for ensuring the smooth flow of goods in and out of a warehouse. However, it involves several complex inventory-related factors, such as order processing, picking, and packing, making the process prone to errors and time-consuming. Fortunately, AI-powered supply chain planning tools can efficiently handle large volumes of data, making the process highly effective. With the ability to analyze massive data sets quickly, these intelligent systems offer timely guidance on forecasting supply and demand. Some advanced AI systems can even predict and detect new consumer trends and forecast seasonal demand, helping to anticipate future customer demands while minimizing the costs of overstocking unwanted inventory. Such AI applications can significantly improve inventory management while reducing errors and costs.


2.WAREHOUSE EFFICIENCY

Optimizing warehouse operations is critical to the success of any supply chain, and AI technology can play a significant role in streamlining warehouse processes. By automating tasks such as item retrieval and delivery, AI can increase efficiency and reduce errors. Furthermore, AI systems are capable of quickly and accurately identifying and resolving warehouse issues, simplifying complex procedures, and accelerating work processes. Not only does this save valuable time, but it also decreases the need for warehouse staff, leading to significant cost savings.


3.ENHANCED SAFETY

The implementation of AI-driven automated tools can result in smarter planning and more efficient warehouse management, ultimately leading to increased safety for workers and materials. Through the analysis of safety data, AI can identify potential risks and inform manufacturers of necessary precautions to take. The technology can also track stocking parameters, provide feedback loops, and facilitate proactive maintenance, allowing companies to quickly respond and maintain compliance with safety standards. By leveraging AI in this way, warehouses can become more secure and ensure the safety of their workers and inventory.


4.REDUCED OPERATIONS COSTS

One significant advantage of AI in the supply chain is its potential to reduce errors and workplace incidents caused by human oversight. AI-based automated systems can operate continuously for extended periods, delivering error-free performance. The use of warehouse robots can lead to faster and more accurate order processing, resulting in higher levels of productivity and lower operational costs.
